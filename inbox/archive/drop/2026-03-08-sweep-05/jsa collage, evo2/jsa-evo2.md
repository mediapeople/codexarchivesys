# JSA Collage — Evolution 02

Second evolution of the JSA collage.

The piece is beginning to move from **fragment collection** toward **structural composition**.

Early layers focused on harvesting imagery from the comics themselves.  
This stage begins introducing **directional force** through diagonals and overlapping planes.

## Observations

Several structural behaviors are emerging:

- Diagonal fragments now act like **load-bearing beams** through the composition.
- Characters remain readable but are being pulled into a **shared visual field**.
- Fragment density is increasing without collapsing legibility.
- The center zone is developing into a **convergence point** for motion and narrative tension.

The rods / shard shapes are beginning to feel like **kinetic vectors**, guiding the eye through the piece.

## Studio Context

The collage is assembled directly on the work table with fragments harvested from comic panels.

Process artifacts remain visible in the workspace:

- loose panel cuttings
- discarded shards
- tools and glue
- surrounding source material

This environment forms part of the work’s ecosystem.

The piece evolves through **incremental placement and removal**, allowing patterns to reveal themselves.

## Reflection

Collage behaves like a **visual signal network**.

Fragments begin as unrelated pieces.  
Through proximity and tension they form a new structure that did not exist in the source material.

At this stage the composition feels less like chaos and more like **controlled fracture**.

The work is not finished.

But the **field is beginning to hold**.

## Archive Note

This entry records **Evolution 02** of the JSA collage.

Future stages may:

- compress the structure further
- reinforce the central energy axis
- remove fragments that weaken the composition
- introduce new visual signals.

The archive preserves each stage so the evolution remains visible.
