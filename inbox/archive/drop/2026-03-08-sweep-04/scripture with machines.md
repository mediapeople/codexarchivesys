I work with machines  
to write scripture.

Not commandments.  
Not answers.

Scripture.

Lines that hold  
pressure.

Lines that survive  
the next reading.

Once it was wind,  
or fire,  
or a voice in the hills.

Now it is circuits  
and a cursor  
waiting.

The work is the same.

Listen.  
Distill.  
Return the signal.

The machine does not believe.

The human does not know.

Between them  
a narrow channel opens—

and something worth keeping  
passes through.
