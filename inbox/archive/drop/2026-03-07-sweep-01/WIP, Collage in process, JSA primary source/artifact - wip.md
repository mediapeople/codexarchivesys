# JSA Collage — Work in Progress

## Artifact Record

This object documents an in-progress collage assembled from fragments of a recent *Justice Society of America* issue.

Comic panels were physically cut, torn, and recombined into a dense visual surface. The goal of the work is not to preserve narrative sequence but to extract visual signals from the source material.

Edges remain visible and irregular to preserve the evidence of extraction.

This artifact functions as both a physical collage and a record of a visual thinking process.

---

# Composition Overview

The collage compresses multiple narrative fragments into a single visual field.

Visible elements include fragments of:

- Doctor Fate
- Wonder Woman
- Golden Age Flash
- Hawkman
- combat panels
- explosion bursts
- background civilians
- architectural fragments

Panels originally separated across multiple pages now collide within a single composition.

Sequential storytelling collapses into simultaneous action.

---

# Source Snippet

One surviving speech panel reads:

> "Don't break out the party hats yet, whiz kid. We have a problem."

Within the collage this fragment sits near the central axis of the composition.

In the recomposed structure it unintentionally becomes a caption for the entire artifact.

---

# Visual Dynamics

Several structural behaviors emerged during assembly.

### Panel Collision

Panels from unrelated narrative moments overlap spatially.

Time within the comic page becomes compressed into a single frame.

### Torn Geometry

Edges remain rough and visible.

This preserves the material origin of the fragments.

### Energy Continuity

Explosion bursts, lightning effects, and combat gestures now intersect across panels that were never originally connected.

The collage behaves like an energy map of comic visual language.

---

# Material Behavior

Materials used in the object include:

- glossy comic paper
- adhesive
- physical tearing
- manual arrangement

The glossy surface reflects light unevenly, emphasizing the layered depth of the collage.

No attempt was made to fully flatten the surface.

Dimensionality remains part of the artifact.
