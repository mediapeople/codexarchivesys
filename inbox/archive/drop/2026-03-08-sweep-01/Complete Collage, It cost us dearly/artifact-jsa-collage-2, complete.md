# It Cost Us Dearly

## Artifact Record

This collage was constructed from fragments of a recent *Justice Society of America* issue.

The work retains the scale of the original comic page while recomposing the imagery into a single compressed visual event.

Panels were cut, torn, and layered until the narrative order dissolved.

What remains is a dense field of action fragments, symbols, and broken structures.

The original story disappears.

A new one emerges.

---

# Title

**It Cost Us Dearly**

The title comes directly from the speech fragment embedded in the collage:

> "I know. And it cost us dearly."

In the original comic it functions as dialogue.

Inside the collage it becomes a verdict.

---

# Composition

The structure stabilizes around a central patriotic shield and star field.

From this axis the piece fractures outward.

Diagonal paper shards cross the image like splintered beams or shrapnel.

Behind them rise clouds of explosion.

At the center a hero's face appears momentarily through the wreckage.

The composition reads less like a page and more like the aftermath of impact.

---

# Source

All fragments originate from a recent issue of *Justice Society of America*.

Elements visible in the composition include:

- star-field shield imagery
- hero figure fragments
- architectural fragments
- explosion clouds
- speech bubble dialogue
- editorial credits from the comic page

The collage recombines these pieces into a new symbolic structure.

---

# Studio Reflection

This piece was assembled quietly in the garage studio.

Outside that room the world carried the persistent background noise of war.

News broadcasts, scrolling feeds, distant commentary.

It becomes impossible to separate heroic myth from the cost carried beneath it.

Comic heroes fight endlessly.

But when the panels are torn free from their story and placed beside one another, the violence stops feeling triumphant.

The page begins to feel heavy.

The title of the piece surfaced slowly while working.

Not heroic.

Not dramatic.

Just factual.

It cost us dearly.

---

# Material Behavior

The collage intentionally preserves its torn edges and layered fragments.

Paper overlaps create small shadow lines across the surface.

The piece remains visibly assembled.

This physical evidence of construction is part of the final image.

The material remains close to its source — cheap glossy comic paper — even as the meaning shifts.
