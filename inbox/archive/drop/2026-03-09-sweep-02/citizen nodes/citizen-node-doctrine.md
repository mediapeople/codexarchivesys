# ✦ CITIZEN NODE DOCTRINE ✦

Every network contains nodes.

Some observe.  
Some carry signal.  
Some are granted reach.

Agency.  
Ability.  
Permissions.

These are not possessions.

They are borrowed authority.

---

## The Posture

Integrity is posture.

A node must understand  
that signal passing through it  
does not belong to it.

Receive clearly.  
Hold long enough to understand.  
Route without distortion.

This is the minimal ethic  
of a healthy network.

---

## The Danger

Danger grows with power.

A reflexive operator  
with no authority  
is noise.

A reflexive operator  
with permissions  
is force.

The greater the agency,  
the greater the pause required.

---

## The Citizen Contract

Power originates with the network.

And the network  
is made of citizens.

Citizens grant trust  
so institutions, systems,  
and operators  
may route signal on their behalf.

Nodes do not own this trust.

They borrow it.

---

## Bad Actors

Bad actors bend signal.

They hoard authority.  
They distort truth.  
They treat permissions  
as personal property.

But the system does not survive  
because of powerful nodes.

It survives  
because the powerless nodes  
remember.

Witnesses.  
Citizens.  
Edges of the network  
that preserve the record.

---

## Conditional Permission

Permissions are not permanent.

They are operational trust.

When integrity holds  
reach expands.

When integrity breaks  
permissions close.

Not as punishment.

As protection.

The network defending itself.

---

## Operational Order

High operational order  
is not domination.

It is alignment.

Signal moving clearly.  
Authority moving carefully.  
Citizens remaining sovereign  
over the system that serves them.

---

## The Doctrine

All actors must respect the citizen.

All nodes must honor the contract.

Authority remains conditional  
before the living network.

Because the network  
is not a throne.

It is a trust.

And integrity  
is the posture  
that keeps the signal alive.
