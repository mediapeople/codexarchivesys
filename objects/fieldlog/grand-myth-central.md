## Arrival

At Charlie's Collectibles.

A quiet cathedral
of toys
and cultural fossils.

My boys drift through the aisles
like small treasure hunters.

Their pockets already emptied
into the mythology.

But they linger anyway.

Places like this
are hard to leave quickly.

## Signal Through Glass

I stand in the aisle

tethered to the net
by a piece of glass.

Music pours softly
through circuitry.

Invisible infrastructure
spanning continents
just to land

in this moment
between shelves.

## Air Channel

My wife calls.

Her voice traveling
through towers
switches
satellites

before landing
directly in my ear.

Two worlds open
at once.

The shop
and the sky.

## Grand Myth Central

This place
is not a store.

It is Grand Myth Central.

Rows of characters
from a thousand stories.

Robots.
Jedi.
Mutants.
Caped gods.

Small pantheons
of late-century myth
lined up
in cardboard temples.

Every aisle
a corridor
through childhood.

Every shelf
a compressed mythology.

## Machine Creature Encounter

Today
I pick up a Transformer.

Red armor.
Yellow chest plate.

The Autobot insignia
burned into the plastic.

The silhouette
instantly recognized.

Rodimus.

## The Birth of Rodimus Prime

There is a moment
in the old story.

Optimus Prime
falls silent.

The Matrix
passed forward.

Hot Rod
young
uncertain
not yet the thing
history requires.

But the light
chooses him anyway.

Energy fills the chamber.

Metal shifts.
Armor expands.

A smaller warrior
becomes something larger.

Rodimus Prime
is born.

## Plastic Echo

Decades later
that same silhouette

exists again
in molded plastic
under fluorescent lights.

The myth
compressed
into a toy.

A few ounces of plastic
holding an entire story.

## The Finds

The artifact count grows.

Megatron appears.

A three-form machine
switching between
military helicopter
and jet.

Then --

Dinobots.

Yes.

Ancient mechanical beasts
from the early wars
of Cybertronian myth.

Dinosaurs
reborn
as machines.

Even here
their silhouettes
carry weight.

## Sideways Story

My youngest tells me
about a former NFL player.

Fame once.

Then homelessness.

Then murder.

The story lands sideways
in the toy aisle.

Rodimus Prime
still standing in my hand.

Plastic heroes
always survive the story.

Humans
do not always
get that ending.

## Expedition Status

The boys are almost ready.

Elijah is talking about
texting grandma.

Reporting the finds.

The money is spent.

But the expedition
was successful.

## Closing the Node

Grand Myth Central
begins to quiet
as we prepare to leave.

Rodimus.

Megatron.

Dinobots.

Small machines
joining the convoy home.

## Field Note

In the span of one visit:

my wife
in the sky channel.

music
in the glass channel.

my sons
in the aisles.

ancient toy myths
under fluorescent stars.

And somewhere in the middle

an operator

standing quietly

watching the old stories
continue to move
through the world.
