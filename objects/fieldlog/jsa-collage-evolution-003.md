This log records the third structural evolution pass of the JSA collage artifact.

The composition has been aggressively reworked through repeated scratches, scoring, shadowing, stamping, and directional tearing across the full field.

The piece now reads less as a collage of panels and more as a compressed mythic debris field.

Structural observations:
- diagonal shard strips behave like load-bearing stress lines converging toward central pressure points
- heroes, gestures, and speech remnants still exist, but they are increasingly buried beneath later layers
- the surface is being treated as terrain rather than composition

Process observations:
- the action pattern became rhythmic: score, shadow, scratch, press, repeat
- hundreds of small interventions accumulated into a denser field of micro-decisions
- panel boundaries were deliberately disregarded in favor of a shared pressure structure

Studio behavior note:

The repeated surface attacks produced an unexpected sense of calm.

Force was applied again and again until the collage settled into a quieter order.

This evolution keeps the artifact open. Plausible next moves include deeper compression, selective removal of weaker fragments, or a decision gate around trim, frame, mount, and scan.
