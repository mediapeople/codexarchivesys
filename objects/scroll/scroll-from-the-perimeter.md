Morning: wires sweating in the walls.
Kettle hiss.
Engine light still on.

Outside, the world performs certainty.
Inside, everything is provisional.

I mark what holds:

A hand on a doorframe
before bad news lands.
A text that says *here*
and means it.
The way breath returns
after naming the fear out loud.

No thunderclap lesson.
No anthem.

Just data from the living field:

People carrying invisible weight
with grocery bags and deadlines.
Nervous systems bargaining with fluorescent weather.
Mercy arriving as small logistics.

At 14:07, despair says *nothing changes.*
At 14:08, someone brings water.
Revision noted.

At dusk, the city hums like old transformers.
Dogs bark at nothing and everything.
A child laughs in the next building
like a match struck in wind.

Conclusion, temporary:

We are not healed.
We are conducting.

Signal passes
through cracked housings,
through tired mouths,
through rooms that smell like bleach and coffee.

Record this clearly:

The current flickers.
The current holds.
