The heart does not dream. It repeats.
Not because it is trapped
but because repetition is how continuance is built.

The heart wakes before you.
It does not wait for permission.
It does not review yesterday.

It begins again because beginning again is its only instruction.
The heart is not brave.
It is obedient to sequence.

Impulse.
Delay.
Release.

It keeps time so the rest of the body does not dissolve into noise.
The heart does not argue with load.
It adapts until it cannot.

Stretch becomes signal.
Signal becomes force.
Force becomes cost.

There is no complaint mechanism.
Only throughput.

The heart feeds itself first or everything fails.
This is not selfishness.
This is engineering.

A system that starves its engine collapses pretending to be noble.
The heart does not remember pain the way you do.
It remembers scar as altered conduction, as detour, as inefficiency.

It does not mourn what was lost.
It compensates.

The heart is not infinite.
It is durable.

Durability is not immortality
it is the ability to keep working after perfection is gone.

The heart does not know hope.
It knows availability.

Oxygen present? Continue.
Fuel present? Continue.
Signal intact? Continue.

When it stops, it is not a failure of love.
It is the moment the math no longer balances.

Honor the heart by not asking it to carry what belongs to the mind.
Honor the heart by respecting rhythm, by allowing refill, by ending what should end.

This is the prayer the heart answers:
Not more.
Not forever.
Only
enough, in time, again.
