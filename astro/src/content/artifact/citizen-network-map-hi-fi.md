This artifact preserves the high-fidelity browser rendering of the Citizen Network map as an object in its own right.

The doctrine text and map logic already live canonically inside `citizen-network-doctrine` and `node-doctrine`. This artifact carries the presentation layer: the amber grid, glowing node plates, chain-routing sequence, and browser-native reading surface that turns the concept map into a digital scroll.

It should be read as a companion implementation rather than a replacement for the doctrine objects.

## Structural Character

- The header stages the doctrine as a signal-bearing invocation instead of a neutral diagram title.
- Concept nodes are rendered as discrete plates with pulse behavior and hover response.
- Relationship chains are arranged as a vertical routing spine rather than a flat flowchart.
- Failure and recovery states are separated into distinct visual blocks, preserving the moral asymmetry of the map.

## Archive Role

This object is useful when the archive needs to preserve not just the language of the doctrine, but the designed reading behavior of the doctrine.

It records the interface argument:

- doctrine can be staged as an experiential surface
- structure can carry emotional tone without losing clarity
- the map is not only referential, it is performative
